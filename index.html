Biography

Ramon Branch Biescas was born in Tarragona, Spain in 2001. Starting music at a young age he attended the choir school Escolania de Montserrat in Catalonia where he had most of his early musical training. There he studied piano with Vicenç Prunés for four years and organ with Merce Sanchís for three years. The choir performed internationally across Russia, France, the United States, Poland, Germany, and China.

He later moved to the UK to continue his musical career and academic studies at Lancing College, receiving organ lessons with Neil Cox and piano lessons with Adrian West. Ramon started the tuba at the age of fourteen with Dave Whitson, and a year later joined the Junior Royal Academy of Music to study with Stephen Wick.

After completing his GCSEs, he attended the Purcell School for Young Musicians to further his career as a tuba player without neglecting his other instruments. He achieved high distinction marks in all his grade 8 ABRSM exams and is working toward diplomas in singing and organ.

Ramon has extensive orchestral experience playing with ensembles such as the London Symphony Orchestra, London Lawyers Symphony Orchestra, Fidelio Symphony Orchestra, Jove Orchestra Nacional de Catalunya, and the Guildhall Symphony Orchestra. He is currently studying with Ben Thomson (Principal Tuba, LSO) and Kevin Morgan (Principal Tuba, RPO).

A keen chamber musician, Ramon plays regularly with the Heron Brass Quintet, who recently reached the semifinal round for the Royal Overseas League competition. Having completed his master's studies, he is freelancing, teaching, and will be joining the Liverpool Philharmonic Scheme to further develop his orchestral career.
