Biography

Ramon Branch Biescas was born in Tarragona, Spain in 2001. Starting music at a young age he attended the choir school Escolania de Montserrat in Catalonia where he had most of his early musical training. There he studied piano with Vicenç Prunés for four years and organ with Merce Sanchís for three years. The choir performed in Russia, France, the United States, Poland, Germany, China and many different areas in Catalonia.

Later he moved to the UK to continue his musical career and his academic studies at Lancing College, where he had organ lessons with Neil Cox and piano lessons with Adrian West. Ramon started the tuba at the age of fourteen with Dave Whitson and a year later joined the Junior Royal Academy of Music to study with Stephen Wick.

After completing his GCSEs, he went to the Purcell School for Young Musicians to continue his musical career as a tuba player without neglecting his other instruments. He achieved a high distinction mark in all his grade 8 ABRSM exams and hopes to achieve his diploma in singing and organ in the future.

Ramon has had experience playing with orchestras such as the London Symphony Orchestra, London Lawyers Symphony Orchestra, Fidelio Symphony Orchestra, Junior Academy Symphony Orchestra, Purcell School Symphony, Jove Orchestra Nacional de Catalunya and the Guildhall Symphony Orchestra. He is currently studying with Ben Thomson, principal tubist at the London Symphony Orchestra and Kevin Morgan, principal tubist of the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra. He also continues with singing lessons and plays organ at various churches.

Ramon is keen on chamber music, playing regularly with the Heron Brass Quintet, who recently reached the semifinal round for the Royal Overseas League competition. Having now completed his master's studies, he is looking forward to freelancing and teaching. He will also be starting the Liverpool Philharmonic Scheme, further developing his orchestral experience and professional growth.
